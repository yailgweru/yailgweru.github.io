## Background
Before AI , there was the tech divide. Billions of people around the world who could not find a chance to enjoy the benefits of technology. Then came AI - bridging that divide wider as AI's capabilities surpass any other technology yet. That has been the case for most Zimbabwean area - especially those out of Harare and Bulawayo.

## A hopeful project

Where other communities find themselves sorounded with a reach mix of professionals , students and institutional support - a brave team of young men and women chose to do the impossible : bring AI to the people that needed most. To represent the city of Gweru and the province of Midlands. Other hubs came and went , but this little hub armed with the belief that small compounding efforts will eventually bring impact. And the case will remain so , until everyone in Gweru is AI literate

## Who have we had yet ?

An interesting question when you think of it? The answer is that over time we have managed to rope in members from different student communities around Gweru. At different times we have partnered with student leads for our programs as presenters and attendees. We have also managed to rope in speakers from different fields and countries - educators , tech professionals and innovators. Then early this year , we first got a place to host our events courtsey of the American Corner Gweru - a place that became our temporary home and included us in their in-house events.

## What's next ?

For the past 18 months , we have found our identity and done trial and error to figure out our position. What follows next is a season of proactive initiatives. We make moves to ensure that in the following months :
- We train as many people as possible in AI
- Collaborate with other organisations
- Work on real , impactful projects that benefit the community

Whether you are new to the AI space of whether you are a pro : our doors are open. Let us come together , share ideas on how we can ensure that we use the power of AI for the benefitof the community. Reach out to us via yail.gweru@gmail.com or our LinkedIn page.
